Other Important Aspects

Symptoms of Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer often shows no early symptoms (up to 75% of cases, INCA). When they appear, they may include:

  • Difficulty urinating or weak/interrupted flow.
  • Frequent urination, especially at night.
  • Pain in the pelvic area, back, or hips.
  • Blood in urine or semen; impotence or painful ejaculation.

Diagnosis and Treatment

Diagnosis is made through PSA tests, rectal exams, and biopsy. Treatments vary by stage: active surveillance, surgery, radiotherapy, hormone therapy, or chemotherapy. Five-year survival rate is 99% for early stages (INCA).
