Prevention and Care

Prostate cancer prevention begins with healthy habits and regular exams. Men over 50 (or 45 with family history) should undergo PSA and rectal exams annually. Risk factors include age, heredity, and obesity (INCA).

Prevention Tips:

  • Healthy diet: Eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and avoid processed meats. Tomatoes (rich in lycopene) may reduce risk by up to 20% (WHO).
  • Physical activity: At least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week, such as walking.
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol: Smoking increases cancer risk; limit alcohol to 2 drinks daily.
  • Control risk factors: Monitor blood pressure and cholesterol; overweight men have 20–30% higher risk.
  • Preventive exams: PSA, rectal exam, ultrasound. Do not ignore symptoms like difficulty urinating.
